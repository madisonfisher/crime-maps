Methodology

We started out by gathering crime data from several cities across the United States: Atlanta, Buffalo, Los Angeles and Philadelphia. In the end, we decided to focus on just Atlanta and Buffalo because there was a lot more data for the two other cities and load times were becoming unreasonable. We selected data from 2010 to 2016 as this is when the cities data overlapped. To finish up our data selection and data clean-up, we removed columns that would have no use in our visualizations. Additionally, the Buffalo data set did not originally contain neighborhoods so one of the Google API packages was used to pull the neighborhoods for each crime based on longitude and latitude.

Our first visualization shows the amount of recorded crime by year in each city. We chose a marker cluster map because it shows a bird's eye view of where crime occurs in the selected. Also, with the marker cluster, one could zoom into a particular area and see a smaller subset of the crime data. When deciding how to visualize the change in crime rates over a particular year, we felt a line graph would be the most clean way to display that change. For our final graph, we chose a stacked bar graph because it easily shows the types of crime and which types were predominant in each city and whether it changed over the years.
